Neutrals — Ink / Paper, swapping roles

Ink #191B21 and Paper #F5F6F7 are the two fixed literals. Every other neutral is color-mix'd from them. In dark mode, Ink becomes the panel/foreground-adjacent surface and Paper becomes the foreground text colour — a true swap, not two independent palettes.

The two ambers — kept deliberately distinct

Gate brass (needs-you, gates only) and message-warning amber (WARNING severity, messages only) must never be mistakable for one another at a glance — one hue, one meaning, per channel (amendment 1, "the amber rule, refined"). Shown together, both modes, to prove the values are genuinely different swatches, not a shared token.

Canonical status palette — entity lifecycle

One system-owned palette for every stateful entity — work units, artifacts, score nodes, chips. Never varies by team; never borrows the message-severity amber.

Message severity — NOTE / WARNING / DANGER

An orthogonal scale answering "how seriously should the Conductor take this message?" — never the entity's lifecycle state. DANGER deliberately reuses the status palette's own red (one meaning, two channels); WARNING is the only place general-purpose amber is permitted, and it is never gate brass.

Motion

Pulse (live things) and blink (in-flight/dispatching) are the only attention-seeking animations. Everything else is fast, physical, quiet. prefers-reduced-motion disables all of it.
