One binary, local-first.

No server to run, no account, no telemetry. It reads your repo and serves a board on localhost. The runner is deterministic — same inputs, same result, byte for byte.

$ levare init .
$ levare serve .

How it works

Capture an idea

02

A team executes

Members declared in a flow do the work — an agent per step, each producing an artifact.

03

Gates halt

Every step ends at a gate. The runner waits. You approve, send back, or reject.

04

The unit ships

The approved artifact lands in your repo — a file, in git, same as any other.

Get it

  • macOS or Linux, arm64 or x64 — ships as a single compiled binary.
  • A git repository — levare reads and writes it directly; there's nowhere else your state lives.
  • A model provider credential, when you want an agent to actually produce something. No key needed to look around — the board, the registry, and every gate work without one.

What it is, and isn't

v0.2.0 · pre-1.0 · built by one person

levare is used daily by its author. The contract, runner, board, sandbox, and MCP remote members are implemented and tested. Expect sharp edges, and expect the schema to move before 1.0. It's Apache-2.0, and the source is the whole story — there's no separate roadmap to read.

  • Not autonomous. Every step waits for you — that's the point, not a limitation.
  • Not hosted. No cloud, no account, no multi-tenant anything — it runs on your machine, against your repo.
  • Not measured in speed. No productivity claims — a gate is a deliberate stop, not friction to advertise around.
  • Not a company. One person builds this, in the open, under Apache-2.0.
